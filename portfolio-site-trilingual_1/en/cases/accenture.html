CONTEXTThe situation

On Google Play’s Greater China (GCN) developer program, I worked as a Business Operations Specialist: identify high-potential developers from reports, and get growth best practices into their hands.

PROBLEMWhat needed solving

My audience was developers — people with a completely different expertise from mine. Handing over a “list of good ideas” changes nothing. Each recommendation had to be translated into the context of their product until it felt like their own problem.

ACTIONSWhat I did

  • Analyzed reports to identify high-potential, high-performing developers
  • Tailored proposals per product: content improvements and online experiments (A/B tests)
  • Reached out to new developers and supported their onboarding end to end
  • Built the GCN content library of best practices, turning one-off insights into a team asset

RESULTSWhat changed

More than 70% of the app developers I pitched enabled the new features, and conversion rates for the games under my management rose by over 10%.

Behind the numbers is one habit: keep translating until it lands. A proposal only creates value when the other person moves — this job made that conviction permanent.
