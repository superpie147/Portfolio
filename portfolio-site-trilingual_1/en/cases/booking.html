CASE 02 · Booking.com (Shanghai) · 2018–2022

CONTEXTThe situation

The company decided to expand the Senior Partner Specialist team — the role that handles escalations. The only thing decided was the goal, “hire them.” How to select and how to train were blank pages.

PROBLEMWhat needed solving

“Hire good seniors” is a vague wish. It had to be translated into criteria you can score and procedures you can repeat. Without criteria, selection runs on interviewer gut feel; without procedures, training is improvised.

ACTIONSWhat I did

  • Selection: defined the competencies a senior needs, designed a written test to measure them, then structured the oral interviews and the candidate feedback process
  • Training: built case-study materials from real escalations, designed the class format, and created a training website for new seniors
  • Rollout: partnered with CS team leads to deliver cross-site training for the newly opened Seoul office
  • Ran cross-site case discussions to lift resolution quality and satisfaction
BEFORE
  • Only the goal “hire them” existed
  • No criteria — selection ran on gut feel
  • No training procedure at all
AFTER
  • A coherent pipeline: written test → interview → feedback
  • Repeatable training built on case studies
  • A system other sites could adopt

RESULTSWhat changed

What began as a one-off hiring effort became a reusable asset: the training site kept serving new-senior onboarding, and the program supported the launch of the Seoul office.

What this proves: the ability to convert a vague goal into measurable criteria and procedures anyone can run.
