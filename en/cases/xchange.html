CASE 01 · Container xChange (container-logistics B2B SaaS) · 2024–2025

Rebuilding a review system
nobody asked me to fix

Problem discoveryRule designTerms & Conditions revisionSOP & help articles
−90%+
review-related support tickets
Discovered and led
end to end
a self-initiated project

CONTEXTThe situation

The platform had a feature letting companies review each other after transactions — but no written rules governed it. Every incident was handled case by case, on each agent’s personal judgment.

PROBLEMWhat needed solving

Handling daily tickets, I noticed a pattern: some users were systematically abusing the feature. No one had asked me to investigate. But left alone, it would erode trust in reviews and keep generating tickets.

I reframed the problem from “individual complaints” to “an absence of rules,” and proposed the fix myself.

ACTIONSWhat I did

  • Mapped the abuse patterns and defined exactly which behaviors to prevent
  • Designed clear operating rules and drove a revision of the Terms & Conditions
  • Documented the entire handling workflow as an SOP
  • Wrote help-center articles so users could resolve issues themselves
BEFORE
  • No rules — every case decided by individual judgment
  • No contractual basis for acting against abuse
  • The same questions kept becoming tickets
AFTER
  • A written SOP anyone can follow consistently
  • Revised terms give clear grounds to act
  • Help articles let users self-serve

RESULTSWhat changed

After the new SOP and help articles went live, review-related support tickets fell by more than 90%. Quality stopped depending on who picked up the case, and the team’s time went to harder problems.

This project is my way of working in miniature: notice the problem no one is looking at, then turn it into a structure anyone can execute.
